# Summary

In an increasingly digital and distributed work environment, companies are confronted with the fragmentation of their collaboration tools: messaging, project management, document sharing, and knowledge access often live in separate applications. This dispersion harms productivity and slows down decision-making.

This end-of-studies project, carried out as part of an internship at **FLESK**, consisted of designing and developing **TEAMNEST**, a collaborative web platform dedicated to teamwork management. The application brings together, in a single secure space, real-time messaging, project and task management, team organization, and an AI-powered intelligent assistant capable of exploiting internal documents.

The platform is built on a modern technology stack: **Next.js** and **React** for the frontend, **FastAPI** (Python) for the backend, **PostgreSQL** for data persistence, **WebSockets** for real-time communication, **Cloudinary** for file storage, and a combination of **Groq** and **Pinecone** to power the AI assistant through a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipeline. Authentication relies on JWT tokens and a role-based access control model (RBAC).

The project was managed following the **Scrum** agile methodology, organized into six successive sprints. Each sprint delivered a functional increment, from authentication and user management to messaging, projects, tasks, documents, and finally the intelligent assistant.

The result is a centralized, responsive, and extensible platform that improves communication and coordination within teams, while making internal knowledge directly accessible through natural language. Future perspectives include a native mobile application, video conferencing, a Kanban board, third-party integrations, and reinforced security through two-factor authentication.
